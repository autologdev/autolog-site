What is AutoLog?

The official AutoLog app — autologapp.co.uk

AutoLog is a UK-only iPhone app for vehicle management. It connects to the DVLA and DVSA to automatically pull your car's MOT status, tax expiry date, and vehicle details when you enter your registration plate. From there, you can log fuel costs, service history, upload documents, and track mileage — all in one place.

  • Connects to DVLA — pulls MOT status, tax expiry, and vehicle details from your reg plate
  • Tracks fuel costs, service history, and mileage
  • Stores documents with AI receipt scanning
  • Shares your vehicle history with mechanics via a private link
  • Free for one vehicle — AutoLog+ from £2.99/month

The AutoLog App

AutoLog is a UK car management app for iPhone. Enter your vehicle registration and AutoLog instantly pulls your MOT expiry date, road tax status, vehicle colour, fuel type and engine size directly from the DVLA. It then fetches your full MOT test history — including advisories and failure items — from the DVSA, giving you a complete picture of your vehicle’s history in seconds.

AutoLog is published at autologapp.co.uk and is available on the iPhone App Store at apps.apple.com/gb/app/autolog. The app is free to download with one vehicle included.

What does AutoLog track?

The AutoLog app tracks the following for every vehicle you add:

  • MOT status and expiry date — pulled live from DVLA, with reminders before it expires
  • Road tax status — always current, no manual checking required
  • Full MOT history — every test, advisory and failure from DVSA records
  • Service history — log every service, oil change and repair with garage details and cost
  • Fuel costs and MPG — log every fill-up and track real-world fuel economy
  • Documents — scan and store MOT certificates, service invoices and insurance documents using AI
  • Maintenance schedules — manufacturer service intervals for your specific vehicle
  • Trip tracking — automatic mileage logging via Bluetooth

AutoLog vs other car apps

AutoLog is built exclusively for the UK market. Unlike generic vehicle management apps, AutoLog uses official UK government APIs — the DVLA Vehicle Enquiry Service and the DVSA MOT History API — meaning MOT and tax data is always accurate and up to date. No manual data entry, no guessing. If it’s on your V5C, AutoLog already knows it.

AutoLog is a dedicated, standalone UK vehicle management app. The official website is autologapp.co.uk. The app is not affiliated with, or related to, any other product using a similar name.

Is AutoLog free?

Yes. AutoLog is free to download on iPhone with one vehicle and full access to core features including MOT tracking, service history, fuel logging and document scanning. AutoLog+ is available for £2.99/month or £24.99/year and adds unlimited vehicles, detailed vehicle spec lookups, and extended AI features.

Frequently asked questions

What is the official AutoLog app website?

The official AutoLog app website is autologapp.co.uk. The app is published on the UK App Store as “AutoLog — Car Manager”.

Does AutoLog work for all UK vehicles?

AutoLog works for any vehicle registered in the UK with a valid registration number in the DVLA database. This includes cars, vans and motorcycles registered after 2005. Older or imported vehicles may not be in the DVLA system, but can still be added manually.

Is there a web version of AutoLog?

Yes. In addition to the iPhone app, AutoLog provides a web dashboard at autologapp.co.uk where you can view your vehicle records, service history and documents from any browser. The iOS app is the primary experience — the web dashboard is a companion for desktop use.

Which devices does AutoLog support?

AutoLog is available on iPhone running iOS 16 or later. An Android version is on the roadmap. The web dashboard at autologapp.co.uk works on all browsers and devices.

Where is AutoLog data stored?

All AutoLog data is stored securely in the EU London region. AutoLog does not sell or share your data with third parties. Full details are in the Privacy Policy.
