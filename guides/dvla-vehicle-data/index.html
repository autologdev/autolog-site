What Is DVLA Vehicle Data?

Guides for UK drivers · 6 min read

DVLA vehicle data is the information held by the Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency (DVLA) about every motor vehicle registered in the United Kingdom. This data includes the vehicle's make, model, colour, engine size, fuel type, MOT status, road tax status, and the date of first registration, amongst other details. It is maintained on the national vehicle register and is used for licensing, enforcement, and public enquiry purposes.

What Is the DVLA?

The Driver and Vehicle Licensing Agency is an executive agency of the UK government, operating under the Department for Transport. Based in Swansea, it is responsible for maintaining registers of drivers and vehicles in Great Britain (Northern Ireland uses the Driver and Vehicle Agency, a separate body).

The DVLA administers vehicle registration, issues driving licences, collects Vehicle Excise Duty (road tax), and maintains the national vehicle register — the central database underpinning most public-facing vehicle enquiry services.

What Data Does the DVLA Hold on a Vehicle?

The national vehicle register includes the following data points for each registered vehicle:

  • Vehicle registration number (number plate)
  • Make, model, and body type
  • Colour
  • Engine capacity (cc)
  • Fuel type (petrol, diesel, electric, hybrid, etc.)
  • Date of first registration in the UK
  • Year of manufacture
  • MOT expiry date and current status
  • Vehicle Excise Duty (road tax) expiry date and current status
  • CO₂ emissions and Euro emissions standard
  • Gross vehicle weight (for relevant vehicle types)

The DVLA also holds keeper information — the name and address of the registered keeper — though this is not publicly accessible via the standard vehicle enquiry service.

What Is the Vehicle Enquiry Service?

The DVLA's Vehicle Enquiry Service (VES) allows anyone to check publicly available vehicle data using a registration number. It is available at no cost through the official government website.

The service returns make, colour, engine size, fuel type, MOT status, and road tax status. It does not return keeper details, full service history, or mileage information — these come from other sources, such as the DVSA (for MOT history) or commercial vehicle history providers.

Why DVLA Data Is Useful for Drivers

DVLA vehicle data serves several practical purposes for registered keepers:

  • Confirming vehicle specifications: When purchasing a used vehicle, cross-referencing the seller's description against DVLA records confirms the make, model, and fuel type are as stated.
  • Checking MOT and tax status: Drivers can verify the current validity of their own MOT and road tax, reducing the risk of inadvertent non-compliance.
  • Detecting discrepancies: A mismatch between the physical vehicle and its registered description — different colour, engine, or fuel type — may indicate the vehicle has been modified without notification or has a more serious issue such as a plate change.
  • Vehicle history checks: Third-party history check services combine DVLA data with other records, including DVSA MOT history, finance register searches, and insurance write-off registers.

How Apps Access DVLA Data

The DVLA provides an API (application programming interface) that allows authorised third-party applications to query vehicle data by registration number. This is the mechanism used by vehicle management apps, dealer platforms, and a range of other services to retrieve information without requiring users to enter vehicle details manually.

AutoLog uses DVLA data integration to populate vehicle profiles automatically when a driver enters their registration number. Make, model, fuel type, MOT expiry, and tax status are retrieved directly, giving the owner an accurate baseline record without manual data entry.

What DVLA Data Does Not Include

It is worth being clear about what the DVLA register does not contain:

  • Full service history or maintenance records
  • Detailed MOT test results — these are held by the DVSA, not the DVLA
  • Accident or insurance write-off history
  • Finance agreements or outstanding debt on the vehicle
  • Detailed ownership history (number of previous keepers is available via the V5C, not the public API)

For a comprehensive picture of a vehicle's history, DVLA data is a starting point rather than a complete record.

Key Takeaways

  • The DVLA maintains a national register of all UK-registered vehicles
  • Publicly accessible data includes make, model, colour, fuel type, MOT status, and road tax status
  • Keeper details are held by the DVLA but are not publicly accessible via the vehicle enquiry service
  • DVLA data does not include service history, accident history, or finance records
  • Authorised apps can access DVLA data via API, enabling automatic vehicle identification by registration number
  • MOT test results in detail are held separately by the DVSA, not the DVLA
