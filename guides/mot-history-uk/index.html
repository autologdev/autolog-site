What Is MOT History in the UK?

Guides for UK drivers · 7 min read

MOT history is the official record of every MOT test a UK-registered vehicle has undergone since May 2005. It is held by the Driver and Vehicle Standards Agency (DVSA) and is publicly accessible using the vehicle's registration number. The record includes pass and fail outcomes, advisory notices, and the mileage recorded at each test.

What Is MOT History?

In the UK, most vehicles over three years old must pass an annual MOT test to confirm they meet minimum road safety and environmental standards. The MOT history is the cumulative record of every test that vehicle has undergone — whether passed, failed, or tested with advisories.

The DVSA began digitising MOT records in 2005. Any test carried out before that date will not appear in the online record, though older certificates may still be held physically.

What Does MOT History Show?

Each entry in a vehicle's MOT history contains the following information:

  • Date of the test
  • Test result — pass or fail
  • Mileage recorded at the time of testing
  • Reasons for failure (if applicable)
  • Advisory notices — items that are not yet dangerous but should be monitored
  • The test station and location
  • Certificate number

Together, this information creates a detailed picture of a vehicle's roadworthiness history over time.

Why MOT History Matters

MOT history is valuable for several reasons, particularly when assessing a used vehicle.

Mileage verification: The mileage recorded at each MOT test creates an independent log of how a vehicle has been driven. Significant discrepancies — for example, a lower reading at a recent test than at a previous one — can indicate that the odometer has been tampered with, a practice known as "clocking". This is a criminal offence in the UK.

Recurring faults: If a vehicle has failed on the same or similar issues across multiple tests, this may indicate an underlying mechanical problem that has not been properly resolved.

Advisory trends: Repeated advisory notices on the same components — brakes, tyres, suspension — can signal developing wear that will eventually require attention.

Maintenance insight: A car with a consistent annual MOT pass record and few advisories suggests it has been well maintained. A patchy history with gaps, failures, or persistent advisories warrants closer scrutiny.

How to Check MOT History in the UK

MOT history can be accessed free of charge through the DVSA's official online service at check-mot.service.gov.uk. All that is required is the vehicle's registration number.

The same information is also accessible via the DVLA's vehicle enquiry service and is used by vehicle history check providers, which combine MOT data with other records such as outstanding finance, write-off status, and ownership history.

Limitations of MOT History

MOT history has practical limitations that are worth understanding:

  • Pre-2005 records: Tests carried out before the DVSA began digitising records are not available online. Older vehicles will have incomplete digital histories.
  • It confirms minimum compliance, not comprehensive maintenance: Passing an MOT does not mean a vehicle is in excellent condition. It means it met the minimum legal standard on the day of the test.
  • Advisories are not failures: Items listed as advisories were not serious enough to fail the test at the time, but they require monitoring and may become more significant.
  • Gaps between tests: A vehicle tested once per year may have developed significant issues in the intervening period that would not be visible in the MOT record.

MOT History and Vehicle Management Apps

For current owners, keeping track of MOT expiry dates is an important part of managing a vehicle. Driving without a valid MOT (where one is required) is a criminal offence and will typically invalidate motor insurance.

Vehicle management apps such as AutoLog retrieve MOT status and expiry dates directly from DVLA data using the vehicle's registration number, providing a clear reminder of when the next test is due. This removes the need to manually track dates across multiple vehicles.

Key Takeaways

  • MOT history is an official DVSA record of every MOT test a UK vehicle has undergone since 2005
  • It shows pass/fail results, advisory notices, failure reasons, and mileage at each test
  • Checking MOT history is free via the government's official service using the registration number
  • Mileage entries can help identify odometer fraud
  • Recurring failures or advisories may indicate unresolved mechanical issues
  • A valid MOT is a legal requirement for most UK vehicles over three years old
